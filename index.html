Projects

Trade&Ahead – Unsupervised Learning

  • Developed model to analyze and group stocks to advise customers on investment strategies.
  • Utilized unsupervised learning techniques of k-means clustering and hierarchical clustering, then compared the models.
  • Observed that companies in the financial sector were the safest investment due to low volatility, high net income, and high median estimated shares outstanding. The model also indicated that companies from the energy sector can be risky investments, due to higher volatility and they appear to be prone to negative price changes.

EasyVisa – Ensemble Techniques

  • Built and tuned multiple classification models to analyze Office of Foreign Labor Certification Data and assist with the process of visa approvals and recommend a suitable profile for the applicants for whom the visa should be certified or denied.
  • Utilized model tuning for decision tree, bagging, random forest, Adaboost, Gradient boost, XGBoost, and stacking, then compared the models. Analyzed the employee's continent, education level, previous job training, previous job experience, intended region of employment, the employer's number of employees, the prevailing wage, unit of wage, if the position was full time or part time, and the case status.
  • Observed the XGBoost model gave the best f1-score of 0.821.
  • Suitable applicants for the Visa program were likely to have attained at least a bachelor's degree, more likely to have previous job experience, unlikely to be paid hourly, likely be from Europe or Africa, and the prevailing wage was likely to be capped at about $200,000.

INN Hotels – Supervised Learning

  • Built and compared two models to analyze booking data to predict hotel bookings that will be canceled in advance and help in formulating profitable policies for cancellations and refunds.
  • Utilized a decision tree and logistic regression models. The bookings' number of adults, number of children, number of weekend nights, number of weeknights, type of meal plan, required parking space, room type, lead time, arrival month, arrival date, market segment, repeated guest, number of previous cancellations, number of previous bookings not cancelled, average price per room, number of special requests and booking status were all analyzed.
  • The pre-pruned decision tree gave a generalized performance with balanced values of precision and recall, an f1-score of 0.809, and could correctly identify 85.7 percent of booking statuses, potentially saving the hotel thousands of dollars per year.
  • Observed that if the lead time is less than or equal to 151 days, then the booking is least likely to be canceled.
